Why Traditional Benchmarks Fail Modern AI Models with OpenAI Research Scientist Noam Brown
TAKEAWAY Traditional AI benchmarks are broken because they don't account for test-time compute. A model's capability is now a function of inference budget — GPT-5.5 can be dramatically more capable at $10,000 than at $10, and we have no framework for evaluating models at the scale they'll actually be used.

The benchmark illusion. Noam Brown explains why GPT-5.5 initially faced skepticism: benchmark grids showed only modest gains over GPT-5.4. But users quickly discovered it was substantially better. The discrepancy: GPT-5.5 is far more efficient with its "thinking" — it achieves superior results with significantly less compute. The benchmarks weren't wrong; they were measuring the wrong thing.

Capability as a function of budget. Modern models have entered a new regime where capability scales with inference budget. Give GPT-5.5 $10 of compute and it performs at one level; give it $10,000 and it can do much more; at $10 million, even more still. The problematic: responsible scaling policies and safety evaluations measure dangerous capabilities at standard budgets, but a model that seems safe at $10 could be dangerous at $10 million. We have no framework for this.

The plateau is too far out. With GPT-5.5, models can productively "think" for weeks before performance plateaus on challenging benchmarks. This makes it impractical to evaluate them at their full potential. Brown proposes plotting model performance as a function of compute budget — a "performance curve" rather than a single benchmark number. But the field hasn't adopted this yet.

Recursive self-improvement and the Erdos conjecture. Brown shares that an OpenAI internal model recently disproved the Erdos unit distance conjecture — a math problem that had resisted human mathematicians. The model did it at a "dirt cheap" budget. This suggests there's significant latent capability in current models that hasn't been explored because the model release cycle (every 2-3 months) is faster than the time needed to push a model to its limits.

基准测试的幻觉。 Noam Brown 解释了为什么 GPT-5.5 最初面临质疑：基准网格显示相比 GPT-5.4 只有 modest 提升。但用户很快发现它实际上好得多。差异在于：GPT-5.5 的"思考"效率高得多——它以显著更少的计算获得更优的结果。基准测试没有错；它们衡量的是错误的东西。

作为预算函数的能力。 现代模型进入了一个新体制，其中能力随推理预算扩展。给 GPT-5.5 10 美元的计算，它表现在一个水平；给它 10,000 美元，它可以做更多；在 1000 万美元时，还可以做得更多。问题在于：责任扩展政策和安全评估在标准预算下衡量危险能力，但在 10 美元时看起来安全的模型在 1000 万美元时可能是危险的。我们没有针对这个的框架。

平台期太远了。 使用 GPT-5.5，模型可以在具有挑战性的基准测试上持续"思考"数周，性能才会进入平台期。这使得在其完整潜力下评估它们变得不切实际。Brown 建议将模型性能绘制为计算预算的函数——一个"性能曲线"而不是单个基准数字。但业界还没有采用这一点。

递归自我改进和 Erdos 猜想。 Brown 分享说，OpenAI 的一个内部模型最近推翻了 Erdos 单位距离猜想——这是一个难住了人类数学家的数学问题。模型以"极其廉价"的预算做到了这一点。这表明当前模型中存在大量未被探索的潜在能力，因为模型发布周期（每 2-3 个月）比将模型推到其极限所需的时间更快。
