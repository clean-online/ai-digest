Anthropic Engineering

An update on recent Claude Code quality reports

Anthropic investigated reports of Claude's responses worsening for some users. Three separate changes were identified and all fixed as of April 20 (v2.1.116): (1) Default reasoning effort changed from high to medium (reverted April 7); (2) A bug caused session context clearing to happen every turn instead of just once after 1 hour idle (fixed April 10); (3) A system prompt instruction to reduce verbosity hurt coding quality (reverted April 20). The aggregate effect looked like broad degradation, but each change affected a different slice of traffic. Key lesson: as models improve, harness assumptions go stale — what was necessary guardrails for Sonnet 4.5 may be dead weight for Opus 4.5.

Anthropic 调查了关于 Claude 对某些用户的响应变差的报告。发现了三个独立的变化，截至 4 月 20 日（v2.1.116）全部修复：（1）默认推理努力从高改为中（4 月 7 日恢复）；（2）一个 bug 导致会话上下文清理在每个回合都发生，而不是在空闲 1 小时后只发生一次（4 月 10 日修复）；（3）系统提示指令减少冗长损害了编码质量（4 月 20 日恢复）。总体效果看起来像广泛降级，但每个变化影响了不同的流量片。关键教训：随着模型改进，harness 假设会变陈旧——对 Sonnet 4.5 必要的护栏对 Opus 4.5 可能是死重。

Anthropic Engineering

Scaling Managed Agents: Decoupling the brain from the hands

Anthropic introduces Claude Managed Agents architecture: decoupling the agent "brain" (session log, orchestration, context management, error recovery) from the "hands" (sandbox execution environment). The insight: this is the same pattern as operating systems virtualizing hardware — process, file, read() are agnostic to whether they access a 1970s disk pack or a modern SSD. Similarly, Managed Agents virtualize agent components (session, harness, sandbox) into stable abstractions that outlast any particular implementation. The post explicitly calls out that "context anxiety" (Claude wrapping up tasks prematurely fearing context limits) is gone in Opus 4.5 — the old harness became dead weight. This is a masterclass in building future-proof AI infrastructure.

Anthropic 介绍 Claude Managed Agents 架构：将 Agent"大脑"（会话日志、编排、上下文管理、错误恢复）与"手"（沙盒执行环境）解耦。洞察：这与操作系统虚拟化硬件的模式相同——process、file、read() 对它们访问的是 1970 年代的磁盘包还是现代 SSD 是不可知的。类似地，Managed Agents 将 Agent 组件（会话、harness、沙盒）虚拟化为稳定的抽象，比任何特定实现都更持久。文章明确指出"上下文焦虑"（Claude 担心上下文限制而过早结束任务）在 Opus 4.5 中消失了——旧的 harness 变成了死重。这是构建面向未来的 AI 基础设施的大师课。

The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck

OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

The Takeaway AI is moving from doing the work we ask of it to autonomously making deep scientific discoveries — and reinforcement learning is the "cake," not the cherry on top, that makes this possible.

Dan Roberts leads the Foundations of Reinforcement Learning team at OpenAI — a team that thinks about the deepest theoretical underpinnings of how AI systems learn from experience. His background is in theoretical physics, which gives him a distinctive intuition for how intelligence emerges from simple rules iterated at scale.

The conversation centers on a remarkable recent milestone: OpenAI, DeepMind, and Anthropic all cracked some of the most famous long-unsolved problems in mathematics known as the Erdős problems — a moment many view as a stunning breakthrough. But Dan is careful to frame this not as "AI solved math" but as AI performing an act of exploration: the model navigated a vast search space of possible approaches, found a promising direction, and then verified its result. This is fundamentally different from brute-force search or even traditional theorem proving.

The deeper insight: reinforcement learning is now "the cake," not the cherry on top. Early LLMs relied overwhelmingly on supervised learning (predicting the next token). RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) was added almost as an afterthought to align outputs with human preferences. But the frontier has shifted: test-time compute, verifiable rewards, and RL-based reasoning are now the primary drivers of capability jumps. The Erdős result is a proof point of this shift.

Dan draws a fascinating parallel to physics: just as thermodynamics provides coarse-grained laws that emerge from statistical mechanics, we may be approaching a "thermodynamics of AI" — principled, predictable relationships between compute, data, model architecture, and reasoning capability. It's a tantalizing prospect: moving from empirical art to scientific engineering.

On the timeline question: Dan believes AI automating AI research is not a distant sci-fi scenario but possibly just a few years away. The combination of better reasoning (via RL), better verification (via formal methods and test-time compute), and better problem formulation (via LLMs understanding scientific intent) creates a plausible path to recursive self-improvement — though we're not there yet.

Dan Roberts 领导 OpenAI 的基础强化学习团队——这个团队思考 AI 系统如何从经验中学习的最深层理论基础的。他的背景是理论物理，这让他对智能如何从大规模迭代的简单规则中出现有独特的直觉。

对话围绕一个非凡的最近里程碑：OpenAI、DeepMind 和 Anthropic 都破解了一些最著名的长期未解决的数学问题，称为 Erdős 问题——许多人认为这是惊人的突破。但 Dan 小心地构建这个问题，不是"AI 解决了数学"，而是 AI 执行了一个探索行为：模型在可能方法的巨大搜索空间中导航，找到了一个有希望的方向，然后验证了它的结果。这与暴力搜索甚至传统定理证明有根本不同。

更深的洞察：强化学习现在是"蛋糕"，而不是上面的樱桃。早期的 LLM 压倒性地依赖监督学习（预测下一个 token）。RLHF（基于人类反馈的强化学习）几乎是事后添加的，以使输出与人类偏好对齐。但前沿已经转移：测试时计算、可验证奖励和基于 RL 的推理现在是可操作性跳跃的主要驱动因素。Erdős 结果是这种转变的一个证明点。

Dan 画了一个迷人的物理学类比：正如热力学提供了从统计力学中出现的粗粒度定律，我们可能正在接近"AI 的热力学"——计算、数据、模型架构和推理能力之间有原则的、可预测的关系。这是一个诱人的前景：从经验艺术转向科学工程。

在时间表问题上：Dan 认为 AI 自动化 AI 研究不是一个遥远的科幻场景，而可能只需要几年。更好的推理（通过 RL）、更好的验证（通过形式化方法和测试时计算）和更好的问题表述（通过 LLM 理解科学意图）的结合，创造了一条通往递归自我改进的合理路径——虽然我们还没有到达那里。
