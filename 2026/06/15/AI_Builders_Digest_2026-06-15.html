Madhu Guru Ex-Google PM (Gemini/Veo)

Madhu offers a deeply empathetic and insightful explanation of what it's actually like to launch a frontier LLM. It's not like shipping traditional software — you're making a decision about a black box with effectively infinite use cases and failure modes. You build extensive evals, red-team, iterate. Then early-access partners still uncover behaviors you didn't anticipate. "You can never be 100% certain you've understood a frontier model." As models get smarter, the launch decision gets harder — for both labs and regulators.

Madhu 对发布前沿 LLM 的实际体验给出了深刻而富有同理心的解释。这不像发布传统软件——你是在对一个实际上有无限用例和失败模式的黑盒做决策。你构建广泛的评估、红队测试、迭代。然后早期访问合作伙伴仍然会发现你没有预料到的行为。"你永远无法 100% 确定你已经理解了一个前沿模型。"随着模型变得更聪明，发布决策也变得更困难——对实验室和监管机构都是如此。

Biohub: The Future of Biology is Open-Source with Mark Zuckerberg, Priscilla Chan, and Alex Rives

The Takeaway Biology is entering its "information theory" era — open-source AI tools for protein/cell modeling could accelerate the cure timeline from "end of century" to decades sooner.

No Priors sits down with Mark Zuckerberg, Priscilla Chan, and Alex Rives — the team behind Biohub's Virtual Biology Initiative. The core thesis: instead of trying to cure diseases directly, build open-source AI tools that let the entire scientific field move faster. They've already folded over 1.1 billion proteins with ESMFold and released it publicly, because the path to progress is through shared tooling, not proprietary silos.

The most striking insight: biology is entering its "information theory" era. Just as LLMs learned the structure of language from data, Biohub is training models that learn the structure of proteins, cells, and eventually whole biological systems. The approach is deliberately hierarchical — you can't model a cell without understanding proteins, and you can't model organ systems without understanding cells. This isn't stamp-collecting; it's building a computable understanding of life itself.

On the regulatory and safety dimension: the team is explicitly not promising to cure all disease. The goal is to accelerate the field. By open-sourcing their models (ESMFold is public), they invite the entire biotech industry and academic community to build on top of their work. The long tail of rare diseases — which get orphaned by traditional pharma incentives — may be the biggest beneficiary, because open tools let any scientist working on a niche condition move faster.

A memorable quote from Priscilla Chan: "If we could design a protein to actually change the physiology, then we can actually cure someone." The gap between "we can predict protein structure" and "we can design a curative protein" is where the next decade of biology will be won. And with AI accelerating both the prediction and the design loop, the timeline for "cure, prevent, or manage all disease by end of century" may be too conservative.

No Priors 本期嘉宾是 Mark Zuckerberg、Priscilla Chan 和 Alex Rives——Biohub 虚拟生物倡议背后的团队。核心论点：与其直接尝试治愈疾病，不如构建开源 AI 工具，让整个科学领域走得更快。他们已经用 ESMFold 折叠了超过 11 亿个蛋白质并公开发布，因为进步的路径是通过共享工具，而不是专有孤岛。

最引人注目的洞察：生物学正在进入它的"信息论"时代。就像 LLM 从数据中学习了语言的结构，Biohub 正在训练学习蛋白质、细胞、以及最终整个生物系统结构的模型。这个方法故意是分层的——不理解蛋白质就无法建模细胞，不理解细胞就无法建模器官系统。这不是在收集邮票；这是在构建对生命本身的可计算理解。

在监管和安全维度：团队明确不承诺治愈所有疾病。目标是加速这个领域。通过开源他们的模型（ESMFold 是公开的），他们邀请整个生物科技行业和学术社区在他们的工作基础上继续构建。罕见病的长尾——被传统制药激励机制忽视的那些——可能是最大的受益者，因为开放工具让任何研究小众病症的科学家都能走得更快。

Priscilla Chan 的一句令人难忘的话："如果我们能设计一个真正改变生理状态的蛋白质，那么我们就能真正治愈一个人。"从"我们能预测蛋白质结构"到"我们能设计治愈性蛋白质"之间的鸿沟，就是未来十年生物学将要取胜的地方。随着 AI 加速预测和设计循环，到本世纪末"治愈、预防或管理所有疾病"的时间线可能过于保守了。
