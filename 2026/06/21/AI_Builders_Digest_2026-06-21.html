Aaron Levie Box CEO

A sharp insight on agent deployment: the main variable in getting success with agents is whether you can get the agent the context it needs to do its work. A major factor is creating a shared working area — a filesystem-shaped interface — that both the person and the agent can work within. Quoting the original post he's responding to: "What they need is a working set: plans, notes, task lists, policies, drafts, summaries, logs, corrections, decisions, etc." Levie argues giving agents access to systems we already know how to use, optimized for them, is the perfect primitive for agent work. This is a key architectural insight for anyone building agent products.

关于 Agent 部署的深刻洞察：Agent 能否成功的关键变量，是你能否给 Agent 提供完成工作所需的上下文。一个重要因素是创建一个人和 Agent 都能工作的共享工作区——一种文件系统形态的接口。引用他回复的原帖："他们需要的是一个工作集：计划、笔记、任务列表、策略、草稿、摘要、日志、修正、决策等。"Levie 认为，让 Agent 访问我们已经知道如何使用、同时针对它们优化的系统，是 Agent 工作的完美原语。这是任何构建 Agent 产品的人都需要理解的关键架构洞察。

Anthropic Engineering Official Blog

Anthropic published a detailed post on how they contain Claude across products — a deep dive into the security architecture that makes it safe to give Claude access to internal systems. Twelve months ago, they'd have rejected out of hand the idea of granting Claude access sufficient to take down an internal service; today that level of access is routine. The post explains the two-component risk model: likelihood of failure and potential damage. The engineering challenge is capping the blast radius. A key revelation: their telemetry showed users approved ~93% of permission prompts in Claude Code — proving that approval fatigue is real and dangerous. This insight motivated the recent launch of Claude Code Auto Mode, which uses model-based classifiers to automate safer approvals and reduce fatigue. The post also mentions Claude Mythos Preview, a model whose blast radius was deemed too high to ship in April 2026, but which may see broader release as safeguards mature.

Anthropic 发布了一篇详细文章，介绍他们如何跨产品限制 Claude 的访问权限——深度探讨了使 Claude 安全访问内部系统的安全架构。12 个月前，他们会断然拒绝给予 Claude 足以搞垮内部服务的访问权限；而今天，这种级别的访问已是常规操作。文章解释了双组件风险模型：故障可能性和潜在损害。工程挑战在于控制爆炸半径。一个关键发现：他们的遥测数据显示，用户在 Claude Code 中批准了约 93% 的权限提示——证明了审批疲劳是真实且危险的。这一洞察推动了最近发布的 Claude Code Auto Mode，它使用基于模型的分类器来自动化更安全的审批并减少疲劳。文章还提到了 Claude Mythos Preview，这是一个爆炸半径被认为过高而未能在 2026 年 4 月发布的模型，但随着防护措施成熟，可能会更广泛地发布。

Re-engineering the Semiconductor Supply Chain with Intel CEO Lip Bu Tan

The Takeaway Intel's new CEO Lip Bu Tan is leveraging the agentic AI boom to revitalize the company, with a philosophy of humble customer focus, product simplification, and strengthening the balance sheet. Jensen Huang's $5B investment (now worth $25B) is a powerful vote of confidence — and a signal that the semiconductor supply chain is entering a new era of strategic importance.

Lip Bu Tan, 66, is not your typical Big Tech CEO. He's a legendary investor (Walden), former Cadence CEO, and now the person tasked with transforming Intel. He took the job not because it was easy, but because Intel is an iconic company and critically important for the US semiconductor ecosystem and national security.

The core business insight: agentic AI is driving massive demand for CPUs, and Intel is positioned to benefit. Tan notes that Jensen Huang, his "all-time friend," invested $5B in Intel — an investment that's now worth $25B. That's a 5x return and a massive strategic endorsement from the most important person in AI hardware.

On his transformation philosophy, Tan emphasizes humility: "First of all you crawl and be humble, listen to customer." His three-step plan: (1) strengthen the balance sheet, (2) focus on and simplify products, (3) drive next-generation leadership products. It's deliberately unglamorous — no grand vision statements, just disciplined execution.

A noteworthy detail: the US government is now a major Intel shareholder. This gives Intel a level of strategic backing that no other US semiconductor company has. The implications for the US-China semiconductor competition are significant and worth watching.

The episode also touches on Tan's investment philosophy: he prefers entrepreneur teams over solo founders, and notes that 9 out of 10 companies he invests in change their business plan halfway because the market changes. Adaptability is everything.

66 岁的 Lip Bu Tan 不是典型的大科技公司 CEO。他是传奇投资人（Walden）、前 Cadence CEO，现在是负责英特尔转型的人。他接下这份工作不是因为它容易，而是因为英特尔是一家标志性公司，对美国半导体生态系统和国家安全至关重要。

核心商业洞察：Agentic AI 正在推动对 CPU 的巨大需求，而英特尔正处于受益位置。Tan 提到他的"老朋友"黄仁勋向英特尔投资了 50 亿美元——这笔投资现在价值 250 亿美元。这是 5 倍的回报，也是来自 AI 硬件领域最重要人物的巨大战略背书。

关于他的转型哲学，Tan 强调谦逊："首先你要学会爬行，保持谦逊，倾听客户的声音。"他的三步计划是：（1）强化资产负债表，（2）专注并简化产品，（3）推动下一代领先产品。这 deliberately 不华丽——没有宏伟的愿景宣言，只有纪律严明的执行。

一个值得注意的细节：美国政府现在是英特尔的主要股东。这让英特尔拥有了其他美国半导体公司都没有的战略支持水平。这对美中半导体竞争的影响值得关注。

节目还触及了 Tan 的投资哲学：他更喜欢创业团队而非单人创始人，并指出他投资的 10 家公司中有 9 家会在中途改变商业计划，因为市场在变。适应能力就是一切。
