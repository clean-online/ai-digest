Madhu Guru Ex-Google Gemini PM

Model routing is genuinely hard — it means mapping each task to the right model, which requires benchmarking against product-specific tasks and dialing in the quality/cost trade-off. Outlines the enterprise progression he witnessed with Gemini: Phase 1 (2024) everyone defaulted to the "it" model regardless of task; Phase 2 (early 2025) teams over-optimized and burned cycles without sophisticated evals; Phase 3 is nuanced routing — the most sophisticated AI-native startups now break their product into sub-agents and route each task to the right model (hardest reasoning to Claude, simplest to Gemini Flash-Lite or open-weight models). Enterprises follow AI-native builders by 6-9 months.

模型路由真的很难——意味着要把每个任务映射到正确的模型，这需要对产品特定任务做基准测试，还要权衡质量与成本。概述了他在 Gemini 亲历的企业演进三阶段：第一阶段（2024 年）所有人默认用"那个"模型，不管什么任务都用它，因为它是最闪亮的新东西；第二阶段（2025 年初）过度优化，团队在没有足够精细 eval 的情况下烧周期，反而交付变慢；第三阶段是精细化路由——最成熟的 AI 原生创业公司已经成功把产品拆成子 agent，把不同任务路由到不同模型，比如最难的推理任务给 Claude，最简单的给 Gemini Flash-Lite 或开源权重模型。企业通常会比 AI 原生建设者晚 6-9 个月跟上这个节奏。

Aaron Levie Box CEO

Token costs are becoming one of the hottest topics for any enterprise he talks with right now. This is very bullish for AI generally because it means these systems are being used at a scale that wasn't contemplated before. It also gives way to another form of differentiation at the applied AI layer: model routing. As tokens take on a significant amount of the cost of any given workflow, companies will inevitably want to ensure their dollars go into the most efficient use of tokens for the particular job at hand. The applied AI layer needs to understand workflows in their domain better than anyone else, and be able to mix and match models to different jobs. This will become one of the bigger differentiation points over time.

Token 成本正在成为他聊过的每一家企业最热门的话题之一。这对 AI 整体来说非常利好，因为这意味着这些系统正在以前所未有的规模被使用。这也为应用层 AI 带来了另一种差异化方式：模型路由。随着 token 在任何工作流成本中占据显著比例，企业不可避免地希望确保他们的每一美元都花在最适合当前任务的 token 使用上。应用层 AI 需要比任何人都更了解自己领域的工作流，并能够将不同模型混合匹配到不同任务上。随着时间推移，这将成为更重要的差异化点之一。

Unsupervised Learning

Ep 89: AI Research Legend's Honest Assessment of Where We Are

The Takeaway Lucas Kaiser, Transformer paper co-author, argues that reasoning alone won't get us to generalization — and that Anthropic's early bet on coding-focused models, while OpenAI was all-in on ChatGPT, is the reason they led the coding wave. The Transformer will persist, but something else that generalizes much better is coming.

Lucas Kaiser is one of the authors of the Transformer paper and has had amazing roles at both Google and OpenAI. On Unsupervised Learning, the host got to ask him all the top-of-mind questions of what's happening in AI today. Of course, they had to talk about the Transformer and how he thinks about its persistence and whether it will remain the dominant architecture and what its shortcomings are.

They also got his thoughts on what changed in the fall to really make coding models so much better and why Anthropic was really first to code. Kaiser's assessment is honest and unvarnished: Anthropic made the very good decision to focus on coding. OpenAI was like, "we're doing ChatGPT." Partway through, Anthropic made this decision because they just could not compete on the general chat front. So they picked coding and owned it. That bet is paying off spectacularly now.

On the question of whether reasoning is enough to get to generalization, or whether another method is needed, Kaiser's intuition is that "it does feel like there is something else that possibly could generalize much better." The Transformer has been incredibly successful, but he sees its limitations clearly. The next architecture may not be a replacement so much as an augmentation — but it will come, and it will generalize better.

On the gap between closed source and open source models, Kaiser thinks it's a fair question but doesn't take a binary stance. The ecosystem will evolve in interesting ways from both open and closed model to application companies. The most interesting dynamics will be at the application layer, where domain-specific knowledge and workflow integration matter more than raw model capability.

Lucas Kaiser 是 Transformer 论文的作者之一，曾在 Google 和 OpenAI 担任重要角色。在无监督学习播客中，主持人得以向他提出所有当下最热门的问题。当然，他们必须聊聊 Transformer，以及他对它持续性的看法——它是否会保持主导架构，以及它的缺点是什么。

他们还讨论了去年秋天发生了什么变化，让编程模型真的变得好那么多，以及为什么 Anthropic 真的在编程方面率先突破。Kaiser 的评估坦诚且不修饰：Anthropic 做了一个非常好的决定，专注于编程。OpenAI 当时的态度是"我们在做 ChatGPT"。某种程度上，Anthropic 做出这个决定是因为他们在通用聊天前端真的竞争不过。所以他们选了编程，并且深耕。这个赌注现在正在获得惊人的回报。

关于推理是否足以实现泛化，或者是否需要另一种方法，Kaiser 的直觉是"确实感觉可能有别的东西能够泛化得好得多"。Transformer 已经取得了令人难以置信的成功，但他清楚地看到了它的局限性。下一个架构也许不是取代，而是增强——但它会来，而且它会泛化得更好。

关于闭源和开源模型之间的差距，Kaiser 认为这是一个合理的问题，但不采取二元立场。生态系统将以有趣的方式从开源和闭源模型演进到应用公司。最有趣的动态将出现在应用层，在那里领域特定知识工作流集成比原始模型能力更重要。
