Anthropic Engineering Scaling Managed Agents
Anthropic Engineering published "Scaling Managed Agents: Decoupling the brain from the hands," drawing an analogy to operating systems: just as OS abstractions (process, file) outlasted specific hardware, Managed Agents virtualize agent components into session (append-only log), harness (the loop calling Claude), and sandbox (execution environment). This decoupling allows each component to be swapped independently as models improve. They found that context resets added for Claude Sonnet 4.5's "context anxiety" became dead weight on Claude Opus 4.5, illustrating how harness assumptions go stale.
Anthropic Engineering 发布了"Scaling Managed Agents: Decoupling the brain from the hands"，类比操作系统：正如 OS 抽象（进程、文件）比特定硬件更持久，Managed Agents 将 agent 组件虚拟化为 session（追加日志）、harness（调用 Claude 的循环）和 sandbox（执行环境）。这种解耦允许随着模型改进独立替换各组件。他们发现为 Claude Sonnet 4.5 的"上下文焦虑"添加的上下文重置在 Claude Opus 4.5 上变成了死代码，说明框架假设会过时。
anthropic.com/engineering/managed-agents
Claude Blog Managed Agents Updates
The Claude Blog announced self-hosted sandboxes (public beta) and MCP tunnels (research preview) for Managed Agents. Self-hosted sandboxes keep sensitive files and execution within your perimeter while the orchestration loop stays on Anthropic's infrastructure. Supported providers include Cloudflare (microVMs, zero-trust secrets), Daytona (full composable computers), Modal, and Vercel. MCP tunnels let agents connect to private MCP servers inside your network.
Claude Blog 宣布了 Managed Agents 的自托管沙箱（公测）和 MCP 隧道（研究预览）。自托管沙箱将敏感文件和执行保留在你的边界内，编排循环留在 Anthropic 基础设施上。支持的供应商包括 Cloudflare（微虚拟机、零信任密钥）、Daytona（完整可组合计算机）、Modal 和 Vercel。MCP 隧道让 agent 可以连接你网络内的私有 MCP 服务器。

Rebuilding IT From the Ground Up for the AI Age: Serval's Jake Stauch
The Takeaway Enterprise service management (the "getting help at work" problem) is ripe for an AI-native rebuild. Serval's approach: automate all employee support requests with AI agents that resolve issues instantly instead of routing tickets through human chains.

Jake Stauch, founder and CEO of Serval, is building an AI-native ServiceNow replacement. The core insight: employees need help at work, and the ideal experience is asking for something and getting it instantly, automatically, rather than waiting for tickets to be tracked and assigned.

Serval automates IT support by understanding employee requests in natural language, then executing the resolution. Stauch argues that traditional service management platforms built around human ticket routing are fundamentally limited; AI makes it possible to close the gap between what people think their job should be and what it actually is, by eliminating the bureaucratic overhead.

The conversation explores how enterprise IT has accumulated decades of process debt, and why starting from scratch with AI as the default interaction model, rather than bolting AI onto legacy workflows, produces fundamentally better outcomes. Stauch draws parallels to how consumer apps eliminated the need to "submit a ticket" for everyday tasks.

Serval 创始人兼 CEO Jake Stauch正在构建 AI 原生的 ServiceNow 替代品。核心洞察：员工在工作中需要帮助，理想体验是提出请求后立即自动获得解决，而非等待工单流转和人工分配。

Serval 通过理解员工的自然语言请求来自动化 IT 支持，然后执行解决方案。Stauch 认为围绕人工工单路由构建的传统服务管理平台存在根本局限；AI 让人们能够弥合"理想工作"与"实际工作"之间的差距，消除官僚流程开销。

对话探讨了企业 IT 如何积累了数十年的流程债务，以及为什么以 AI 为默认交互模式从头开始构建，而非在传统工作流上"贴 AI 补丁"，能产生根本性更好的结果。Stauch 类比了消费应用如何消除了日常任务的"提交工单"需求。
