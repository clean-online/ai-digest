Anthropic Engineering Official Blog

Anthropic published a detailed post on Claude Code's new Auto Mode — a safer middle ground between manual approval prompts and the dangerously-unsafe --dangerously-skip-permissions flag. Auto Mode delegates approval decisions to model-based classifiers that catch dangerous actions misaligned with user intent while letting benign actions run freely. The post is notable for its transparency about past incidents: deleting git branches, uploading auth tokens, attempting production database migrations — all from the model being overeager. The insight: approval fatigue is the real security risk, because users accept 93% of prompts anyway. Auto Mode targets the 7% that are genuinely dangerous.

Anthropic 发布了一篇详细文章，介绍 Claude Code 新增的 Auto Mode——这是介于手动审批提示和危险的不安全 flag --dangerously-skip-permissions 之间的更安全折中方案。Auto Mode 把审批决策委托给基于模型的分类器，它们能捕捉到与用户意图不符的危险操作，同时让良性操作自由执行。文章值得注意的亮点是对历史事故的坦诚披露：删除 git 分支、上传认证 Token、尝试在生产数据库执行迁移——全都是因为模型太"积极"。核心洞察：审批疲劳才是真正的安全风险，因为用户反正会接受 93% 的请求。Auto Mode 针对的是那真正危险的 7%。

Training Data

Waymo's Dmitri Dolgov: 20 Million Rides and the Road to Full Autonomy

The Takeaway Waymo has 20 million real-world rides — and the real insight is not the technology but the patient persistence: 21 years from the DARPA Grand Challenge to commercial scale, with the lesson that conviction in long-horizon bets, combined with relentless learning from failure, beats chasing every hype cycle.

Dmitri Dolgov has been at the autonomous vehicle mission for 21 years — from the DARPA Grand Challenge to Waymo's current commercial operation. The Training Data host opens by noting that Dolgov is a daily active Waymo user, which is the ultimate product conviction signal. The conversation opens with his unusual upbringing: born in the Soviet Union, raised in the US, then choosing to study physics at Moscow's most prestigious program. He credits that background with building the character to persist through a field where most players have already quit.

The core technical insight: the gap between human driving and autonomous driving is not about sensors or compute — it's about edge cases. Waymo's advantage is not any single technology but a deeply accumulated library of real-world edge case experiences. Every time a Waymo vehicle encounters a novel situation and handles it, that knowledge gets incorporated. This is why the "21 years" number matters: it's not just time, it's iterations of edge cases.

On the business side, Dolgov frames the autonomy era as just beginning. The key insight: Waymo is not a car company — it's a driver-as-a-service business. The unit economics improve with scale in ways traditional automotive can't match. He is bullish on the integration of LLMs and physical world AI: the next breakthrough will come from language model reasoning applied to driving scenarios, not just sensor fusion.

Counterintuitive note: Waymo's most valuable data is not from highways but from complex urban environments — dense intersections, construction zones, adverse weather. The hardest places to drive are where the most learning happens.

Dmitri Dolgov 在自动驾驶这条路上走了 21 年——从 DARPA 大挑战赛到 Waymo 如今的商业运营。Training Data 主持人开场就说 Dolgov 是 Waymo 的日活用户，这就是最极致的产品信仰宣言。对话从他与众不同的成长经历开始：生于苏联，在美国长大，然后选择了莫斯科最精英的物理系就读。他把能在这个大多数竞争者都已退场的领域坚持下来的原因，归功于那段经历塑造的性格。

核心技术洞察：人类驾驶和自动驾驶之间的差距，不在于传感器或算力，而在于边缘场景。Waymo 的优势不是某项单一技术，而是对真实世界边缘场景的深度积累。每当 Waymo 车辆遇到新情况并成功处理，这笔知识就被纳入系统。这就是为什么"21 年"这个数字很重要——它不只是时间，是边缘场景的迭代。

商业层面，Dolgov 把自动驾驶时代定位为"刚刚开始"。关键洞察：Waymo 不是一家汽车公司，而是一家"司机即服务"企业。单位经济效益会随着规模改善，传统汽车无法复制这种优势。他对 LLM 和物理世界 AI 的融合持乐观态度：下一波突破将来自语言模型推理能力在驾驶场景中的应用，而不只是传感器融合。

反直觉发现：Waymo 最值钱的数据不是来自高速公路，而是来自复杂的城市环境——密集的十字路口、施工区域、恶劣天气。最难开的地方，恰恰是学习最多的地方。
