Andrej Karpathy Ex-Tesla AI / OpenAI Founding Team

A practical workflow tip with a deep claim baked in: at the end of any LLM query, ask it to "structure your response as HTML" and view the file in your browser. He's also had success asking models to present output as slideshows. The bigger thesis: audio is the human-preferred input to AIs, but vision (images / animations / video) is the preferred output. Roughly a third of the brain is a massively parallel processor dedicated to vision — it's the 10-lane superhighway of human information intake. Karpathy is essentially nudging the field: stop dumping walls of markdown text on people and start rendering structured visuals.

一个简单到容易被忽视的工作流技巧，背后却藏着一个大判断：在任何 LLM 查询的末尾加一句"把回复结构化为 HTML"，然后用浏览器打开生成的文件。他也成功用过让模型把输出做成幻灯片。更重要的论断是：音频是人类向 AI 输入的首选方式，而视觉（图像 / 动画 / 视频）才是 AI 向人类输出的首选方式。大脑大约三分之一是专门处理视觉的大规模并行处理器，是人类信息吸收的"10 车道高速公路"。Karpathy 实际上是在推动行业：别再把大段 markdown 文本砸给用户，开始渲染结构化的视觉内容。

Amex Global Business Travel: The World's First AI Take-Private — with Long Lake CEO Alexander Taubman

The Takeaway Long Lake is buying Amex Global Business Travel for $6.3B in what may be the world's first AI take-private. The thesis is not "cut costs with AI" — it's "make existing teams 30-40% more productive, then grow like a software company in industries that historically grew 0-5% a year." Roll-up + AI is the new playbook.

Alex Taubman, co-founder and CEO of Long Lake Management, just announced an agreement to acquire Amex Global Business Travel — the world's largest corporate travel platform — for $6.3 billion. It's likely the largest "AI take-private" deal to date. Long Lake has already done ~30 prior acquisitions, all under the same thesis: buy real-economy service businesses (HOAs, and now three other verticals) and transform them with AI via their internal Nexus platform.

Taubman is explicit about what makes this strategy different from the standard PE roll-up: this is not a cost-cutting play, it's a growth play. The traditional Silicon Valley move would be "build software for HOAs, sell it as a vendor." Long Lake's bet is that you get better outcomes with deeper alignment by actually owning the company — software vendors and customers have inherently misaligned incentives, owners and customers don't.

The numbers behind the thesis are striking. Pre-acquisition, his portfolio companies were typically growing 0-5% a year in volume. Post-AI integration, they're now growing 20%+ a year, organically. The mechanism: Nexus gives team members instant time savings (often 25-30% of their day back), which Long Lake reinvests into customer acquisition and service quality rather than headcount cuts. Customer retention goes up, response times drop, board reporting / budgeting / email errors fall — and the businesses start looking like software companies on the income statement.

The most interesting structural insight is about labor as a marginal tax on growth. In labor-intensive service industries, growing 20% historically meant hiring 20% more people — find them, train them, manage them — and you only kept ~20¢ of every incremental revenue dollar because the rest went to incremental labor. AI breaks that math. Once teams are 30-40% more efficient, incremental margins look like SaaS, and growth flips from "painful and dilutive" to "the most attractive use of capital." That, more than any single AI feature, is why Long Lake can pay $6.3B for a sleepy travel business and expect software-style returns.

A subtle talent moat falls out of the same loop: once an employee gets used to AI-augmented workflows, going to a competitor means doing 25-30% of their day in mundane work again — Taubman compares it to giving up email. Long Lake is now a talent magnet in industries that previously had high churn, can pay above market because productivity per head is higher, and watches the flywheel compound.

Long Lake Management 的联合创始人兼 CEO Alex Taubman 刚刚宣布，将以 63 亿美元收购全球最大的企业差旅平台 Amex Global Business Travel。这很可能是迄今为止规模最大的"AI take-private"（AI 驱动的私有化收购）。Long Lake 此前已经完成约 30 起并购，逻辑都一样：买下实体经济里的服务型企业（最初是 HOA 物业管理，现在又扩展到另外三个垂直行业），然后用他们内部的 Nexus 平台对其进行 AI 改造。

Taubman 把这套打法和传统 PE 卷王式 roll-up 做了切割：这不是降本，是增长。传统硅谷的玩法是"为 HOA 行业做一套软件、当 vendor 卖给他们"。Long Lake 的判断是：直接拥有这家公司，能形成更深的利益对齐，效果更好——软件供应商和客户之间天然有利益错位，而股东和客户之间没有。

支撑这套论断的数字很扎眼。被收购前，Long Lake 旗下的公司业务量年增长率通常只有 0-5%。AI 整合之后，这些公司现在的有机增长率达到 20% 以上。机制是：Nexus 让一线员工每天瞬间省出 25-30% 的时间，Long Lake 把这部分产能重新投入到获客和服务质量上，而不是裁员。结果是客户留存率上升、响应速度变快，董事会汇报 / 预算 / 邮件出错率下降——这些业务在利润表上开始长得像软件公司。

整集播客最深的结构性洞察是：在劳动密集型行业里，劳动力本质上是"对增长征收的边际税"。过去每增长 20%，就要再招 20% 的人——找人、培训、管理样样都来；而每多挣一块钱，最后只能留下大概 20 美分，剩下的全被增量人力吃掉。AI 把这道数学题推翻了。当现有团队效率提升 30-40%，增量利润率瞬间像 SaaS 一样漂亮，"增长"也从"痛苦且稀释股权的事"变成"最值得投钱的事"。这才是 Long Lake 敢于花 63 亿美元买一家"沉睡的差旅公司"，并期待它跑出软件级回报的真正原因——比任何一个具体的 AI 功能都更关键。

同样的飞轮还顺手挖出了一条人才护城河：员工一旦习惯了 AI 增强的工作流，跳去竞争对手就意味着每天又要花 25-30% 时间做杂活——Taubman 形容那种感觉就像让你戒掉电子邮件。Long Lake 因此在传统上人员流动率很高的行业里，反而成了人才磁石：因为人均产出更高，所以能开出超出市场的薪酬，飞轮不断加速。
