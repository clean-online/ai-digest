Anthropic's Boris Cherny: Coding's Printing Press Moment

The Takeaway The creator of Claude Code hasn't written a single line of code in 2026. His job has shifted entirely to directing thousands of agents, and he believes coding is about to become universal literacy, not a specialized skill.

Boris Cherny built Claude Code and has had a front-row seat to the reinvention of software development. The most striking fact: he wrote textbooks on TypeScript, spent his entire career writing code, and hasn't manually written a single line in 2026. His workflow is now entirely about directing agents. At night, he runs "thousands" of agents in parallel, and during the day he reviews and steers their output.

His core thesis: coding is experiencing its "printing press moment." Before the printing press, literacy was a specialized skill for monks and scribes. After it, reading and writing became universal. The same thing is happening to programming. The barrier between "having an idea" and "building software" is collapsing. Within a few years, anyone who can describe what they want will be able to create software, just as anyone who can think of something to say can write it down.

The practical implications for developers: your job is shifting from writing code to specifying intent, reviewing output, and managing agent workflows. Boris now uses Claude Code almost exclusively from his phone, dictating what he wants built. The skill that matters is clarity of thought and the ability to break complex systems into well-defined pieces that agents can execute.

Counterintuitive insight: Boris argues that this doesn't eliminate developers. It massively increases demand for software, because the bottleneck was never "not enough coders" but "too expensive to build." When building costs approach zero, the amount of software the world wants will explode.

Boris Cherny 创造了 Claude Code，亲眼见证了软件开发的重塑。最震撼的事实是：他写过 TypeScript 教科书，整个职业生涯都在写代码，但2026 年至今没有手写过一行代码。他的工作流程已完全转变为指挥 agent。夜里，他同时运行"数千个" agent；白天则审查和引导它们的产出。

他的核心论点：编程正在经历它的"印刷术时刻"。印刷术出现之前，读写是僧侣和抄写员的专属技能。之后，它变成了人人必备的基础能力。编程正在经历同样的事。"有想法"和"做出软件"之间的壁垒正在坍塌。几年之内，任何能描述自己想要什么的人都能创建软件，就像任何能想到话要说的人都能把它写下来一样。

对开发者的实际影响：你的工作正在从写代码转向明确意图、审查产出、管理 agent 工作流。Boris 现在几乎完全在手机上用 Claude Code，口述他想构建的东西。真正重要的技能是思维清晰度，以及把复杂系统拆解为 agent 可执行的明确单元的能力。

反直觉洞察：Boris 认为这并不会消灭开发者。它会大幅增加对软件的需求，因为瓶颈从来不是"程序员不够"，而是"构建太贵"。当构建成本趋近于零，世界对软件的需求量将呈爆炸式增长。
