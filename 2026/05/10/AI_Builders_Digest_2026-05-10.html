AI & I by Every

The Secrets of Claude's Platform From the Team Who Built It

The Takeaway Anthropic's platform team reveals that Claude's evolution from a completion endpoint to a self-optimizing cloud platform is not just a product story — it's a fundamental rethinking of what a "model company" means: the platform must be smart enough to understand and reconfigure itself, or the agents built on top of it will hit a ceiling the models alone cannot break through.

Dan Shipper sits down with Angela Destiny and Caitlin — Anthropic's Head of Product for Cloud Platform and Head of Engineering for Cloud Platform — for a rare inside look at what it actually means to build a "platform" in the AI era.

The conversation opens with the long arc of what "platform" has meant at Anthropic. In the GPT-3 days, the platform was a completion endpoint — you sent a prompt, got a response. Then came tool calling, chat sessions, and session state persistence. Now, with Cloud Managed Agents, you're essentially getting "a cloud on a computer with memory." The through-line: each step added more statefulness, more agency, more infrastructure the model company has to manage on the customer's behalf.

The most striking vision: Claude figuring out what model you should be using and how to spin up sub-agents — on the fly, without you thinking about architecture. The dream is a platform where Claude understands itself well enough to rewrite its own configuration for the task at hand. This requires serious scale, but the direction is clear: from "prompt completion" to "self-optimizing AI cloud."

A key insight on what differentiates a model company's platform from a traditional cloud: it's not just infrastructure, it's intelligence. Traditional cloud gives you compute; Anthropic's platform gives you compute plus a model that can reason about how to use it. That changes the competitive dynamics entirely — the moat isn't just model quality, it's platform intelligence.

On the commercial model question that Matt Turck was also asking about today: the platform team is grappling with how to price something that's simultaneously compute, intelligence, and workflow. Consumption-based pricing is the current answer, but the tension between token costs and enterprise needs (identities, budgets, audit logs) is very much an open question they're actively thinking through.

Dan Shipper 与 Anthropic 云平台产品负责人 Angela Destiny 和工程负责人 Caitlin 对谈，这是难得的内部视角，来看"在 AI 时代构建平台"到底意味着什么。

对话从 Anthropic 语境里"平台"一词的漫长演变轨迹开始说起。GPT-3 时代，平台就是一个补全接口——发个 prompt，拿个回复。然后有了工具调用、聊天会话和会话状态持久化。现在，Cloud Managed Agents 本质上相当于"一台带记忆的云端电脑"。贯穿其中的脉络：每一步都在增加状态性、主动性和基础设施——模型公司必须替用户管理的东西越来越多。

最引人注目的愿景：Claude 自己判断该用哪个模型、该启动哪些子 Agent——即时完成，无需你思考架构。理想状态是一个 Claude 对自己的理解足够深，以至于能为手头任务动态重写配置的 AI 云。这需要极大的规模，但方向已经清晰：从"补全 prompt"到"自我优化的 AI 云"。

关于模型公司平台与传统云的关键差异：不只是基础设施，更是智能。传统云给你算力；Anthropic 的平台给你算力加一个能推理如何使用它的模型。这将竞争逻辑完全改变——护城河不只是模型质量，而是平台智能。

关于定价模式——Matt Turck 今天也提了这个问题：平台团队正在权衡如何给一种同时是算力、又是智能、又是工作流的东西定价。消耗式定价是当前答案，但 Token 成本与企业需求（身份、预算、审计日志）之间的张力，是一个他们正在认真思考的开放问题。
