Aaron Levie Box CEO

Box is hiring for AI Automation Engineering roles — and Levie explains why in depth: deploying advanced agents into knowledge work beyond coding is genuinely hard. You need to wire up systems securely, give agents the right data context, ensure quality output, design human-in-the-loop workflows, and maintain agents through model upgrades. This post crystallizes a new job category that's emerging across the enterprise: AI automation engineers as a permanent function, not a one-off experiment.

Box 正在招聘 AI Automation Engineering 岗位——Levie 详细解释为什么：把高级 Agent 部署到编程以外的知识工作场景，真实地困难。需要安全地接入系统、给 Agent 配置正确的上下文数据、确保输出质量、设计人机协同的工作流、还要在模型升级时维护好 Agent。这不是副业项目——这是面向关键业务流程的前沿工程师工作。这条帖子清晰地定义了一个正在企业里兴起的新职业类别：AI 自动化工程师作为常设职能，而非一次性尝试。

Anthropic Engineering Official Blog

Anthropic published a deep-dive on scaling Managed Agents — the core insight: decouple the brain from the hands. The blog explains how they virtualized the three components of an agent (session, harness, sandbox) so each can be swapped independently as models improve. The key analogy is operating systems: the read() command doesn't care if it's a 1970s disk pack or an SSD — and Managed Agents should be equally agnostic about which model runs the reasoning. A concrete example: Claude Sonnet 4.5 needed explicit context-reset instructions in the harness; Claude Opus 4.5 didn't — but if they'd hardcoded those resets, they would have become dead weight. Virtualization lets the harness evolve with the model.

Anthropic 发布了一篇关于扩展 Managed Agents 的深度文章——核心洞察：把大脑和手解耦。文章解释了如何将 Agent 的三个组件（session、harness、sandbox）虚拟化，使每个组件在模型升级时都可以独立替换。核心类比是操作系统：read() 命令不关心底层是 1970 年代的磁盘组还是现代 SSD——Managed Agents 也应该对运行推理的具体模型保持中立。一个具体案例：Claude Sonnet 4.5 需要在 harness 里显式加 context-reset 指令；Claude Opus 4.5 则不需要——但如果当初硬编码了那些 reset，后来就成了无效代码。虚拟化让 harness 可以随模型一起进化。

No Priors

Baseten CEO Tuhin Srivastava on the AI Inference Crunch, Custom Models, and Building the Inference Cloud

The Takeaway The inference market is not a winner-take-all commodity game — it's a services business where deep customer relationships, specialized models, and "long tail" enterprise demand create durable moats. Baseten's 30x growth in a year proves that the real opportunity is helping enterprises own their inference rather than being locked into expensive closed-source APIs.

Tuhin Srivastava founded Baseten to ride the AI inference wave — and in the past year, the company grew 30x and expects to hit $1B+ in revenue. The conversation starts with the observation that open-source models have crossed a critical capability threshold, and post-training techniques have become mainstream enough that enterprises are increasingly taking their inference in-house. "The long tail of models is coming true" — a diverse set of specialized models serving specific enterprise needs, not just one or two frontier models dominating everything.

The infrastructure insight: inference is a services business, not a commodity play. GPU clusters are a commodity; customer trust, model expertise, and workflow integration are not. Baseten's competitive position comes from deeply understanding what their customers are running — and being able to suggest the right model for the right job, with the right serving infrastructure behind it. The key competitive moat is not hardware but accumulated knowledge of production inference patterns.

On the multi-chip future: Baseten runs on diverse hardware (H100s, custom silicon, emerging options), and Tuhin is explicit that no single chip will dominate. The right inference infrastructure is workload-dependent — some tasks are best on GPUs, some on custom silicon, some on CPUs. The implication for enterprise buyers: don't commit to a single hardware vendor for AI inference — the landscape is too dynamic.

A contrarian note: the biggest enterprise AI winners over the next five years won't be the companies with the best models — they'll be the companies that figured out how to embed AI into workflows with durable customer relationships. The inference layer is just plumbing. The moat is in the relationship and the domain-specific fine-tuning that makes the model genuinely useful for a specific use case.

Tuhin Srivastava 创立 Baseten 是为了押注 AI 推理浪潮——过去一年公司增长 30 倍，预计年内营收突破 10 亿美元。对话从开放模型跨越关键能力门槛的观察开始：后训练技术已经主流化，越来越多的企业开始把推理工作收归内部。"长尾模型的时代正在到来"——各种各样的专业化模型服务于特定企业需求，而非一两个前沿模型包打天下。

基础设施层面的洞察：推理是一个服务业务，而非大宗商品生意。GPU 集群是大宗商品；客户信任、模型专业能力和工作流集成则不是。Baseten 的竞争优势来自深度理解客户在跑什么——并能给出建议：什么任务该用什么模型，背后需要什么样的服务基础设施。真正的护城河不是硬件，而是对生产推理模式积累的深层认知。

关于多芯片未来：Baseten 运行在多种硬件上（H100、定制芯片、新兴选项），Tuhin 明确表示不会有单一芯片称霸。正确的推理基础设施取决于工作负载——有些任务在 GPU 上跑最好，有些在定制芯片上，有些在 CPU 上。对企业买家的启示：不要把 AI 推理押注在单一硬件供应商上——格局变化太快。

一个反直觉观点：未来五年最大的企业 AI 赢家，不会是模型最好的公司，而是那些弄清楚了如何将 AI 嵌入有持久客户关系的工作流的公司。推理层只是管道。护城河在关系里，在让模型对特定场景真正有用的领域定制化微调里。
