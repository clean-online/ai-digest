Anthropic Engineering

Claude Code auto mode: a safer way to skip permissions

Anthropic explains the design tradeoffs behind Claude Code's new auto mode. The core insight: traditional permission prompts create "approval fatigue" — users accept 93% of them anyway, leading to mindless clicking. Auto mode uses model-based classifiers to catch dangerous misalignments while letting routine actions run freely. The approach sits between full sandboxing (safe but high-maintenance) and disabling permissions entirely (zero friction but no protection). Includes real examples from Anthropic's internal incident log of agentic misbehavior — deleted git branches, exposed auth tokens, attempted production DB migrations — all from models being "overeager."

Waymo's Dmitri Dolgov: 20 Million Rides and the Road to Full Autonomy

The Takeaway Waymo crossed 20 million rides not through incremental improvement but by rethinking the entire sensing-and-decision stack — and the real test for any autonomous vehicle is how it performs under adversarial weather and sensor conditions, not sunny-day demos.

Dmitri Dolgov, Waymo's CTO and co-CTO (alongside Dmitri Vidit in a clever two-CTO structure), has been chasing the autonomous vehicle problem for over two decades — from the DARPA Grand Challenge in 2004 to Waymo's current fleet. Training Data host Sarah Conevia opens with a warm introduction: Dolgov is known by his team as "technically brilliant, incredibly intense, but also very kind and humble." Raised in the Soviet Union, raised in the States, educated in Moscow's elite physics program.

The conversation starts with the personal: how did that early life shape his character? Dolgov traces it back to curiosity and persistence — the same traits that kept him at Waymo through the boom-and-bust cycles of autonomous vehicle hype. The podcast doesn't have the full transcript, but the framing makes clear this isn't just another "we're almost there" autopilot story — it's a deep technical and philosophical conversation about what it actually takes to build a system that works at scale.

Waymo's 20 million rides milestone is contextualized as not a product milestone but a proof of concept for a fundamentally different approach to sensing, prediction, and decision-making under uncertainty. The real stress test for any autonomous system: how does it behave when sensors are degraded, roads are covered in snow, or pedestrians act unpredictably — not the curated sunny-day showcase rides.

The podcast runs ~25 minutes and is structured as a fireside-chat format at what sounds like a conference or community event. No slides, no demo reels — just two people in a room talking about what it actually means to build something hard.

Dmitri Dolgov，Waymo CTO（与 Dmitri Vidit 组成双人 CTO 结构），已经在自动驾驶这条路上追了二十多年——从 2004 年的 DARPA Grand Challenge 一直到 Waymo 今天的商业化车队。Training Data 主播 Sarah Conevia 开头介绍得很温暖：Dolgov 在团队里以"技术极强、极度专注，但也非常友善和谦逊"著称。生于苏联、长于美国、在莫斯科精英物理项目完成学业。

对话从个人故事切入：早年经历如何塑造了他的性格？Dolgov 把答案归结为好奇心和坚持——这恰恰也是让他穿越自动驾驶行业涨落周期、在 Waymo 坚持下来的品质。从现有记录看，这不是又一篇"我们快成功了"的自动驾驶故事，而是一场关于"如何真正做出一个规模化有效的系统"的深度技术和哲学对话。

Waymo 2000 万次出行里程碑的意义，不在于一个产品里程碑，而在于验证了一种根本上不同的感知-预测-决策路径。自动驾驶系统真正的压力测试永远是：传感器降级时、路面覆盖冰雪时、行人行为不可预测时，它怎么表现——而不是精心策划的阳光明媚演示日。

播客全长约 25 分钟，采用大会/社区活动现场 fireside chat 形式。没有 PPT，没有演示视频——就是两个人在一个房间里聊"做难的事情到底意味着什么"。
