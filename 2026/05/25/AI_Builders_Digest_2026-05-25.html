Aaron Levie Box CEO

Aaron Levie pushes back on the "AI will eliminate all jobs" narrative with a nuanced frame: we constantly confuse task completion with eliminating the whole job. Even as AI automates one or many tasks within a job, the definition of the job almost inevitably expands — to do vastly more of those tasks, do them at higher quality, or move on to tasks that haven't been automated yet. The result: the job becomes valuable in a new way, often to an entirely new audience. This applies to coding, legal, sales, marketing — and Levie argues it's why jobs aren't going away in the way some predict.

Aaron Levie 用一个细致的框架反驳"AI 将消灭所有工作"的叙事：我们不断把"任务完成"和"消除整个工作"混为一谈。即使 AI 自动化了工作中的一项或多项任务，工作的定义几乎不可避免地会扩展——去做更多此类任务，以更高质量完成它们，或者转向尚未被自动化的任务。结果：这份工作以一种新的方式变得更有价值，通常面向全新的受众。这适用于编程、法律、销售、营销——Levie 认为，这就是为什么工作不会以某些人预测的方式消失。
