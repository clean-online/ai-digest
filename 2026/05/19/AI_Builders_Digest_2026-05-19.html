Two major threads. First: there's a temporary mismatch between the jobs CS graduates used to chase (building consumer-facing software at tech companies) and the new reality — AI makes coding abundant, which means every industry now needs technical talent to implement agentic systems. The expert with AI always outruns the novice with AI. The experienced developer using agents outpaces someone just vibe coding. The designer who uses AI builds better products. Despite valley rhetoric that deep expertise is obsolete, the opposite is true.

两条重磅推文。第一条：CS 毕业生曾经趋之若鹜的岗位（在科技公司做面向消费者的软件）和新的现实之间存在暂时错位——AI 让编程变得充裕，意味着每个行业都需要技术人才来部署 Agent 系统。一位财富 500 强药企 CEO 告诉他，他们需要更多技术人才。技能的需求还在，只是岗位描述变了。第二条：别放弃学基本功。专家+AI 永远胜过新手+AI。有经验的开发者用 Agent 比纯 vibe coding 的人效率高得多，会用 AI 的设计师做出的产品更好。硅谷有人说深度专业已过时，但恰恰相反。

AI & I by Every

Claude Code Can Be Your Second Brain

The Takeaway The real breakthrough in AI-assisted thinking isn't about writing — it's about thinking mode. By running Claude Code against your entire Obsidian vault on a home server accessible from your phone, you can research, reason, and ship from anywhere. The key discipline: explicitly tell the AI you're in thinking mode, not writing mode — and build daily progress reports into your workflow.

Noah Breyer has what might be the most advanced personal Claude Code setup in the world. He runs it on his Obsidian vault — 120k+ markdown files synced via Git — hosted on a mini PC in his basement, accessible from his phone through Tailscale VPN and the Termius terminal app. The result: deep research, writing, and even shipping code from a device that was never designed for deep work.

The core insight: separate thinking mode from writing mode. Breyer explicitly tells Claude Code "don't help me write anything right now, I just want you to help me think and ask me questions." Most people let AI jump straight to producing artifacts, but the highest-leverage use is as a thinking partner — one that can search your entire knowledge base, pull relevant research, and challenge your assumptions.

His workflow for a new project: create a folder, tell Claude about past work and big themes, have it search the entire Obsidian vault for relevant material, then switch to a "thinking partner" sub-agent that asks probing questions rather than generating output. At the end of each day, he has the AI write a progress report — what was learned, what ideas emerged, what to explore next.

The broader philosophy: building Fingerspitzengefühl — "fingertip feeling" — is what matters most with AI. You can't reason about these tools without experiencing them. The intuition gap is the real divide, not the knowledge gap. And for code sharing between projects, Breyer and his team use AI to translate approaches across repos instead of building abstracted modular libraries — a pattern he calls "tacit code sharing."

On AI and kids: rather than banning AI, teach children the meta-skill of detecting when the AI is wrong. Hallucination detection is just a form of the critical thinking needed for all media consumption. The "brain guard" rule: if information makes you feel good because you agree with it, be even more skeptical.

Noah Breyer 可能拥有世界上最先进的个人 Claude Code 设置。他在 Obsidian 知识库上运行它——超过 12 万个 Markdown 文件通过 Git 同步——托管在地下室的 mini PC 上，通过 Tailscale VPN 和 Termius 终端 app 从手机访问。结果：在一个从不为深度工作设计的设备上，做研究、写作，甚至推送代码。

核心洞察：把"思考模式"和"写作模式"分开。Breyer 明确告诉 Claude Code"现在不要帮我写任何东西，我只要你帮我思考、问我问题"。大多数人让 AI 直接产出成果，但最高杠杆的用法是作为思考伙伴——一个能搜索你的整个知识库、拉取相关研究、挑战你假设的伙伴。

他的新项目工作流：创建文件夹，告诉 Claude 过去的工作和大主题，让它搜索整个 Obsidian 找相关材料，然后切换到"思考伙伴"子 Agent 来提深入问题而不是生成输出。每天结束时，让 AI 写一份进度报告——学到了什么、冒出了什么想法、接下来探索什么。

更广的哲学：建立 Fingerspitzengefühl——"指尖感觉"——才是 AI 时代最重要的。你不能在没有体验这些工具的情况下推理它们。直觉差距才是真正的鸿沟，不是知识差距。在项目间共享代码方面，Breyer 和团队用 AI 在不同 repo 之间翻译方法，而不是构建抽象的模块化库——他称之为"隐性代码共享"。

关于 AI 和孩子：与其禁止 AI，不如教孩子检测 AI 何时出错这个元技能。幻觉检测只是所有媒体消费都需要的那种批判性思维的一个形式。"大脑卫士"规则：如果某条信息让你因为赞同而感觉良好，那你就应该更加怀疑它。
