Garry Tan Y Combinator CEO

A sprawling philosophical thread attacking Foucault and Derrida as the intellectual root of America's inability to build. Foucault's framework — that every institution claiming knowledge is really exercising power — when applied at civilizational scale, kills the tacit process knowledge that only exists in the hands of people who actually make things. Derrida's deconstruction becomes the OS behind bureaucratic paralysis: 4,000-page environmental impact reports but no concrete poured. The antidote is building: "The physical bridge across a river holds or it doesn't." Also announced GBrain as free, open-source under MIT license — a knowledge system (not RAG) with 8 layers that makes agents "feel clairvoyant about who you are." Installable in one command.

一条长篇哲学推文，直指福柯和德里达是美国"丧失建造能力"的思想根源。福柯的框架——任何声称拥有知识的机构实质上都在行使权力——一旦在文明尺度上应用，就会杀死只存在于实干者手中的默会过程知识。德里达的解构主义则成了官僚瘫痪的操作系统：能写出 4000 页环境影响报告，却倒不了混凝土。解药就是建造："桥过河，要么撑住，要么塌，没有第三种可能。"此外还宣布 GBrain 以 MIT 协议免费开源——它是一个知识系统（不是 RAG），8 层架构让 Agent"对你了如指掌"。一行命令即可安装。

Pax Silica: Inside the Trump Administration's Tech Strategy with US Under Secretary of State for Economic Affairs Jacob Helberg

The Takeaway Pax Silica is the US government's attempt to build an AI supply chain NATO — a 14-nation economic security coalition that bets on private-sector dynamism over government-operated infrastructure, with the Philippines as the first forward-deployed industrial base. The bet: commercially viable platforms that outlive any single administration.

Jacob Helberg, now confirmed as Under Secretary of State for Economic Affairs, outlines Pax Silica — an economic security coalition of 14 countries designed to secure the AI supply chain for the US and its allies. The philosophical foundation is distinctly American: "We're not gonna do government-operated supply chains — that's not how we shine as a country. Our superpower is really our private sector." The Steve Jobs quote "American products enchant and delight users around the world by the billions" serves as the guiding principle.

The first major "product rollout" is a forward-deployed industrial base with the Philippines — America's oldest ally in Asia. The idea: instead of reshoring everything, create distributed manufacturing nodes in allied nations that are commercially viable and can survive changes in political administration. This is infrastructure designed to outlast any single president.

On the chip export control front, Helberg argues the current framework is evolving. The key insight: controlling advanced AI compute is not about restricting trade — it's about shaping the ecosystem so that the most capable infrastructure stays within a trusted circle. The Pax Silica countries get preferential access; those outside face increasingly tight constraints.

Counterintuitive note: Helberg explicitly rejects the "industrial policy" label. He frames Pax Silica as the opposite — government enabling private companies to compete, not picking winners. The test: "Can this platform ultimately live outside the government as a private service?" If yes, it's aligned with the model. If it requires permanent government subsidy, it's not.

已获确认的美国经济事务副国务卿 Jacob Helberg 详细阐述了 Pax Silica——一个由 14 国组成的经济安全联盟，旨在为美国及其盟友保障 AI 供应链。哲学基石是鲜明的美国路线："我们不会搞政府运营的供应链——那不是我们国家的强项。我们真正的超能力是私营部门。"乔布斯那句"美国产品让全球数十亿人为之着迷"被奉为指南。

第一个重大"产品发布"是与菲律宾建立的前沿部署工业基地——菲律宾是美国在亚洲最古老的盟友。核心思路：不是把所有制造都搬回美国，而是在盟国创建分布式的制造节点，这些节点在商业上可行，能跨越政府更迭存活下来。这是为超越任何单个总统任期而设计的基础设施。

在芯片出口管制方面，Helberg 认为当前框架正在演进。关键洞察：管控先进 AI 算力不是限制贸易——而是塑造生态，让最强大的基础设施留在可信的圈层内。Pax Silica 成员国获得优先准入；圈外国家面临越来越严的约束。

反直觉之处：Helberg 明确拒绝"产业政策"的标签。他把 Pax Silica 定位为反面——政府赋能私营企业竞争，而非挑选赢家。检验标准："这个平台最终能否脱离政府作为私营服务存在？"如果可以，就符合模型；如果需要永久政府补贴，就不符合。
