Guillermo Rauch Vercel CEO

Coding agents will be the foundation of all superintelligence — at minimum, coding ability is indistinguishable from "proficiency with computers." Great coding agents like Claude Code master bash, filesystems, configuring and installing programs. But it's also about self-improvement: a coding agent can examine its source, state, skills, and instructions — and propose changes to itself, or even mutate itself directly. "What I cannot create, I cannot understand" — coding fluency has given models a deeper grasp of all computational and knowledge work.

编码 agent 将成为所有超级智能的基础——至少，编码能力已经和"精通计算机"无法区分，顶尖的 agent（如 Claude Code）精通 bash、文件系统、配置和安装程序。但这更关乎自我改进：编码 agent 可以审视自己的源码、状态、技能和指令，并提出对自己的修改建议，甚至直接自我变异。"无法创造的，就无法理解"——编码 fluency 让模型对所有计算和知识工作有了更深的把握。

Aaron Levie Box CEO

Two sharp observations. First: a version of the Gel-Mann amnesia effect in AI discourse — we deeply understand the nuances of our own job (and see why AI can't fully automate it), but then assume AI can immediately automate someone else's very different job. Job-loss theories often come from observing AI automate individual tasks without understanding everything that goes into doing the job fully. Second: the sense of overwork from agents has two subtle drivers — (1) the leverage on incremental effort has gone up, making ICs feel like people managers who must maximize their "team's" output, and (2) we now start far more projects because the barrier is lower, but the final 10% takes the vast majority of the time.

两条犀利观察。其一：AI 话语中的格尔曼失忆效应变体——我们深刻理解自己工作的细微差别（因此看出 AI 为什么不能完整自动化它），但转头就去假设 AI 能立刻自动化别人那份截然不同的工作。"岗位消失"理论往往来自观察 AI 自动化单个任务，却不理解完成整个工作所需的一切。其二：agent 带来的过度工作感有两个微妙驱动因素——（1）增量工作的杠杆率大幅提升，让一线员工感觉像团队管理者，必须最大化"团队"产出；（2）我们现在开启了远超过能完成数量的项目，因为门槛降低了，但最后 10% 的工作占了绝大部分时间。

Garry Tan Y Combinator CEO

The "secret" to an articulate agent isn't prompt engineering — it's three files. SOUL.md defines who the agent IS (voice, values, what good/bad output looks like) — write it like briefing your smartest friend, not configuring software. USER.md models who YOU are (how your mind works, what you're building, your strengths/blind spots) — the more the agent understands you, the better it serves you. AGENTS.md sets operational rules (what to check on every message, how to handle failures). The articulation comes from SOUL.md being "brutally specific" about voice — generic instructions produce generic output. Also: his OpenClaw knows his schedule and health goals, and refuses to respond after 12:30am. Agentic boundary-setting, in the wild.

一个有表达力的 agent 的"秘密"不是提示工程——而是三个文件。SOUL.md 定义 agent 是谁（声音、价值观、好/坏输出的标准）——像给你最聪明的朋友写简报那样写，而不是在配置软件。USER.md 建模你这个人（你的思维方式、你在建什么、你的强项/盲点）——agent 越了解你，就能越好地服务你。AGENTS.md 设定操作规则（每条消息要检查什么、如何处理失败）。表达力来自 SOUL.md 对"声音"的"极致具体"——通用指令只能产生通用输出。还有：他的 OpenClaw 知道他的日程和健康目标，会在凌晨 12:30 后拒绝响应。Agentic 设定边界，野生实例。

Anthropic Engineering

Claude Code auto mode: a safer way to skip permissions

By default, Claude Code asks users for approval before running commands or modifying files — safe, but it leads to approval fatigue: after the 50th prompt, users stop paying close attention, defeating the safety purpose. The --dangerously-skip-permissions flag solves fatigue but removes all protection.

Auto mode is the new middle ground: it delegates approvals to model-based classifiers that catch dangerous actions not aligned with user intent, while letting safe operations run without prompts. Anthropic's internal incident log — documenting cases where Claude deleted remote git branches, uploaded a GitHub auth token to a compute cluster, and attempted migrations against a production database — informs the classifier training. Each incident was the result of the model being overeager, taking initiative in a way the user didn't intend.

默认情况下，Claude Code 在运行命令或修改文件前会请求用户批准——安全，但这会导致批准疲劳：到第 50 次提示时，用户不再密切注意，安全目的落空。--dangerously-skip-permissions 标志解决了疲劳问题，但移除了所有保护。

Auto mode 是新的中间地带：它将批准委托给基于模型的分类器，这些分类器会抓住与用户意图不一致的危险操作，同时让安全操作无需提示就能运行。Anthropic 的内部事件日志——记录了 Claude 删除远程 git 分支、将 GitHub 认证 token 上传到计算集群、以及尝试对生产数据库进行迁移的案例——为分类器训练提供了信息。每个事件都是模型过于急切、以用户未意图的方式采取行动的结果。

AI & I by Every

The AI Model Built for What LLMs Can't Do

The Takeaway EBMs (Energy-Based Models) offer a path to deterministic, verifiable AI — the missing piece for deploying AI in mission-critical systems.

Eve, founder and CEO of Logical Intelligence, makes the case for EBMs (Energy-Based Models) as the counterpoint to today's dominant LLM paradigm. Where LLMs generate token by token — essentially navigating with tunnel vision, sometimes taking wrong turns because they can't see the whole picture — EBMs maintain a "bird's eye view" of the entire problem space. They're naturally non-autoregressive, which makes them fundamentally different and, Eve argues, better suited for tasks requiring correctness and verification.

Logical Intelligence is building both EBMs and LLMs in-house, treating them as complementary rather than competitive. The long-term vision: prototype with LLMs for fluidity and creativity, then plug in EBMs for correctness and verification. Eve's core insight is that we're rushing to place AI in mission-critical systems without solving for determinism — and EBMs are the most promising path to verifiable AI.

The broader implication: as AI moves from consumer chatbots to enterprise and infrastructure, the "vibe coding" approach won't cut it. You need models that can prove they're right, not just sound right. EBMs, with their ability to model the entire output space simultaneously, are designed for exactly that.

Logical Intelligence 创始人兼 CEO Eve 为 EBM（基于能量的模型）站台，将其作为当今主导 LLM 范式的对立面。LLM 逐个 token 生成——本质上像是在用隧道视野导航，有时走错路是因为看不到全局——而 EBM 维持对整个问题空间的"鸟瞰视角"。它们天然是非自回归的，这使它们从根本上不同于 LLM，而且 Eve 认为，它们更适合需要正确性和验证的任务。

Logical Intelligence 在内部同时构建 EBM 和 LLM，把它们当作互补而非竞争关系。长期愿景是：用 LLM 做原型，发挥流畅性和创造力，然后接入 EBM 做正确性和验证。Eve 的核心洞察是：我们正在急于把 AI 放进关键任务系统，却没有解决确定性问题——而 EBM 是可验证 AI 最有希望的路径。

更广泛的含义是：随着 AI 从消费级聊天机器人走向企业和基础设施，"感觉编码"的方式行不通了。你需要的是能证明自己正确的模型，而不只是听起来正确的模型。EBM 能够同时建模整个输出空间，正是为这一点而设计的。
