Aaron Levie Box CEO

AI gives extraordinary leverage in almost any domain — meaning we're at a unique moment where ambitious people with core skills can overcome historical experience requirements. Junior employees can now do far more than even senior ones could a couple years ago, and the people who take advantage will get massively ahead. Companies should find these "from the future" employees and put them in key positions.

AI 在几乎任何领域都带来了前所未有的杠杆效应——我们正处于一个独特的历史时刻，有野心和核心技能的人能够跨越传统的"经验门槛"。今天的初级员工能做的事，比几年前的资深员工还要多得多，谁先用上 AI 谁就能甩开同行。公司应该主动寻找这些"来自未来"的员工，把他们放到关键岗位上。

No Priors

Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott

The Takeaway Replacing enterprise platforms with raw LLM-generated code costs 10× more than running a dedicated workflow platform — and people will never forgive software for non-deterministic mistakes the way they forgive humans.

ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott (formerly SAP CEO, who started by buying a deli at 16) makes a sharp counter-argument to the "SaaSpocalypse" thesis. His core insight: the cost of replacing a mature enterprise platform with LLM-generated code is enormous when you add up GPU spend, token costs, human capital reallocation, and the missing context built up over decades of customer relationships. By his math, replicating a simple ServiceNow application via a language model costs 10× more — and when it makes a mistake (which LLMs do, since they're not deterministic), there's no one to call.

His framing for the next era: "AI thinks, workflow acts." ServiceNow positions itself as the AI control tower that integrates hyperscalers, language models, and 800+ systems of record into a coherent agentic business. The Armis acquisition extends this into IT/OT security — managing not just software but networks, manufacturing tools, medical devices, and physical infrastructure. They integrated Armis in 20 days.

Counterintuitive insight on enterprise risk: the companies most exposed to generative-code disruption are single-function departmental tools with low CEO-priority. Platforms that span multiple departments and own a true system of record are insulated by switching costs and contextual data depth.

Implementation speed is now a moat: major customers go live in <30 days, vs. multi-year SaaS rollouts of the financial-crisis era. SIs aren't dying — they're doing more, smaller, faster projects.

Personal note: McDermott does 17 one-on-ones with quota-carrying reps in a single morning, with 72 more on this month's calendar. His leadership maxim: "When the tide goes out, you want to be fully dressed."

ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott（前 SAP CEO，16 岁靠盘下一家熟食店起步）对"SaaS 末日论"提出了一个犀利的反驳。他的核心洞察是：把成熟的企业平台用 LLM 生成的代码替换掉，把 GPU 算力、Token 成本、人力机会成本、加上几十年客户关系积累的上下文一起算进去，代价惊人。按他的测算，用语言模型复制一个简单的 ServiceNow 应用，成本要高 10 倍——而且当它出错时（LLM 不是确定性的，必然出错），你连个能找的人都没有。

他对下一个时代的概括："AI 负责思考，工作流负责行动。" ServiceNow 把自己定位为 "AI 控制塔"——整合超大规模云厂商、各家语言模型和 800 多个系统记录方，构成一个连贯的 agentic 业务。Armis 收购把这个能力延伸到 IT/OT 安全——管理的不只是软件，还有网络、制造业工具、医疗设备和物理基础设施。整合 Armis 只用了 20 天。

关于企业风险的反直觉洞察：最容易被生成式代码颠覆的，是单一功能的部门级工具，尤其是 CEO 优先级低的那些。跨部门、握有真正系统记录方的平台，靠切换成本和上下文数据深度形成天然护城河。

落地速度本身就是护城河：现在大客户上线只要 30 天以内，而不是金融危机时代动辄数年的 SaaS 实施周期。系统集成商（SI）也不会消亡——他们做的项目变得更多、更小、更快。

个人侧面：McDermott 一个早上能和 17 位一线销售开 17 场一对一，本月日程上还有 72 场。他的领导箴言："潮水退去时，你最好穿戴整齐。"
