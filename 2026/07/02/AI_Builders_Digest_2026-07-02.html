AI & I by Every
AI & I by Every · Hosted by Dan Shipper
The AI Workflows Behind Every's Consulting Team
TAKEAWAYIn the AI era, you can build anything — the real question is whether you should build and maintain it. AI agents excel at executing SOPs, but still need human management for taste, judgment, and surfacing what matters in the data.
Natalia, head of consulting at Every, walks through how their internal AI agent "Claudie" has evolved from a vibe-coded Google Sheets CRM glue into a real operations system with its own LinkedIn, Twitter feed, dashboards, and a self-evaluation loop. Claudie now runs on a trust battery — continuously self-assessing performance and improving based on feedback.

The key pivot: despite being able to vibe-code their own CRM, Every moved to Atio (a professional CRM) and Asana (project management). Natalia's reasoning is sharp: "You can build anything in the AI era — the question is, should you build and maintain it?" Professional tools handle data quality at a scale that a vibe-coded system can't sustain. Claudie excels at executing against standard operating procedures, but two things still require humans: constant managerial oversight for taste and excellence, and someone to surface the interesting signals in the data.

She also shares a delightful personal use case: using Claude artifacts to create on-the-go guides during a trip to New Orleans — including reading her Spotify playlists to recommend which bands to see at French Quarter Fest, and creating bilingual guides to share with her mom. Knowledge work, she says, is becoming like gardening: you create the conditions for growth, but you don't make the plant with your hands.
Natalia，Every 咨询业务负责人，讲述了内部 AI agent "Claudie" 如何从一个用 Google Sheets 胶水粘起来的 vibe-coded CRM，进化成一个真正的运营系统——拥有自己的 LinkedIn、Twitter 账号、数据看板，以及自我评估反馈循环。

关键转折：尽管完全有能力 vibe-code 自己的 CRM，Every 最终转向了 Atio（专业 CRM）和 Asana（项目管理）。Natalia 的理由一针见血：「在 AI 时代你可以构建任何东西——问题是：你应该构建和维护它吗？」专业工具在数据质量维护上的规模效应是 vibe-coded 系统无法持续的。Claudie 擅长执行标准操作流程（SOP），但两件事仍然需要人类：持续的管理监督来确保品味和卓越，以及有人从数据中提炼出有意思的信号。

她还分享了一个个人用例：旅行中用 Claude artifacts 创建实时导览——包括读取她的 Spotify 播放列表来推荐 French Quarter Fest 该看哪些乐队，以及创建双语导览和妈妈分享。她说，知识工作正在变得像园艺：你创造生长的条件，但你不是用手在造那棵植物。

Aaron Levie CEO @ Box
Levie highlights Devin's "agentic mapreduce" approach — fanning out focused agents over bounded shards of code, reducing findings into one report, then verifying vulnerabilities in isolated sandboxes. He argues this is why we'll need 100x more AI inference in the future: swarms of agents processing massive datasets for tasks humans never could. The key enabler is deploying a mix of frontier and lower-cost models, because of the sheer token volume involved. This will be a major value proposition for the applied AI layer across pharma, banking, and any industry dealing with unstructured data.
Levie 重点介绍了 Devin 的「agentic mapreduce」方法——将专注型 agent 分散到代码的不同分片上，将发现汇总成一份报告，然后在隔离沙箱中验证漏洞。他认为这就是未来 AI 推理需求将增长 100 倍的原因：agent 群处理海量数据集，完成人类根本无法完成的任务。关键使能因素是同时部署前沿模型和低成本模型，因为涉及的总 token 量极其庞大。这将成为应用 AI 层在制药、银行等任何处理非结构化数据行业中的核心价值主张。
