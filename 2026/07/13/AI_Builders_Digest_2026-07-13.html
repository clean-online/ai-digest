Swyx AI Engineer / Latent Space

Swyx extends the Jevons Paradox to the agentic-engineering era. His core claim: as labor efficiency rises and the unit cost of knowledge work falls broadly, total demand for work — and for better knowledge — goes UP, not down. The coding surge isn't an exception to this pattern; it's the herald of it spreading to all knowledge work as coding agents "break containment" into other domains. The humans who win are "AI Engineers" — those who can wield coding agents well.

Swyx 把杰文斯悖论（Jevons Paradox）延展到了智能体工程时代。他的核心判断：随着劳动力效率提升、知识工作的单位成本普遍下降，对"工作量"和"更好的知识"的总需求反而会上升，而不是下降。 coding 这一波不是例外，而是前兆——当 coding agent 突破边界、蔓延到其他知识工作领域，同样的规律会重演。能赢的人是"AI 工程师"——那些能熟练驾驭 coding agent 的人。

Aaron Levie Box CEO

Aaron Levie makes the labor-abundance case. Software job postings are outpacing other fields, he notes — the opposite of the "AI replaces software jobs" expectation. His reasoning is Jevons-style: when you lower the unit cost of production for something with many use cases, demand rises, so people launch more software projects than ever. The job isn't fully automated (and won't be for the foreseeable future), so you still need people to maintain, decide, and run the systems. "Agents will cause more abundance than replacement."

Aaron Levie 给出了"劳动力增量"的论证。他指出软件岗位招聘量正在超过其他行业——与"AI 取代软件工作"的预期恰恰相反。他的逻辑是杰文斯式的：当某样用途广泛的东西的单位生产成本下降，需求就会上升，于是人们比以往启动更多软件项目。这个岗位不会被完全自动化（可预见的未来也不会），所以仍需人来维护、决策和运维系统。"Agent 带来的增量，会大于替代。"

No Priors

Travel Through the Lens of AI with Booking.com CEO Glenn Fogel

The Takeaway An incumbent's calm view of the AI wave: there is no moat, only relentless reinvention — yet scale, regulation, and a real agentic product (Penny) are already bending the curve. The harder question he raises is social, not technical: how to reskill people faster than the jobs disappear.

Glenn Fogel, CEO of Booking Holdings (27 years in, from Priceline's dot-com-crash lows to a ~$130–180B company), opens with a blunt thesis: "There is no such thing as a moat." He tells his 25,000 employees daily that today's advantages can vanish tomorrow; the only long-term win is continuously developing new services. He sees clear parallels to the 1999–2000 boom (~1,500 IPOs, ~2 dozen left) and expects a similar washout this AI cycle — alongside real value created.

On AI and travel, Booking's mission is "make it easier for everybody to experience the world," and LLMs make that cheaper and better for both travelers and partners. Its agentic tool Penny (Priceline) has doubled adoption monthly for months, lifting conversion, speeding search, shortening the path to booking, cutting cancellations, and raising customer-success rates — though still tiny against $186B booked last year. The open problem is token economics: cost per trip, ROI, lifetime value, and which model to use for which purpose.

Customer service is the clearest win: AI cut cost-per-contact ~10% and lifted satisfaction, with the balance left to customers who still want a human. Capital allocation runs ~$700M into AI/platform this year, plus a long record of returning cash — $3.6B buyback in Q4 alone, ~40% of shares repurchased over ~12 years — when no better internal use exists.

On durable advantage: Booking holds 8.6M alternative-accommodation listings (~¾ of Airbnb in that segment, growing faster there for 5 years) plus a large hotel business; global travel's rising regulation is a barrier new entrants underrate. Fogel's worry is jobs: tech has always replaced work (Booking's 40-language human translation is gone to machine translation), but the speed — and the people who can't reskill, his "50-year-old truck driver" — is the real risk. He advocates upskilling staff to be "AI literate" and warns against societies rejecting tech from fear, noting China isn't held back that way. His call: an honest, non-doomer conversation.

Booking Holdings CEO Glenn Fogel（任职 27 年，见证 Priceline 从互联网泡沫谷底走到约 1300–1800 亿美元市值）开门见山："根本不存在护城河。"他每天对他的 2.5 万名员工说：今天的优势明天就可能消失；长期唯一的胜算是持续推出新服务。他认为当下与 1999–2000 的泡沫高度相似（约 1500 家公司 IPO、仅余约 24 家），预期这轮 AI 周期也会有类似的出清——但同时也会创造真实价值。

论及 AI 与旅行，Booking 的使命是"让每个人都能更轻松地体验世界"，而大语言模型让这件事对旅客和合作方都更便宜、更好。其智能体工具 Penny（Priceline）过去数月月活翻倍，提升了转化率、加快了搜索、缩短了预订路径、降低了退订、提高了客服成功率——尽管相对去年 1860 亿美元的预订额仍很小。悬而未决的问题是token 经济：单次行程的成本、ROI、用户生命周期价值，以及不同用途该用哪个模型。

客服是最明确的赢面：AI 把单次联系成本降了约 10%、满意度反而上升，仍把"想找真人"的选择权留给用户。资本配置上，今年约投入 7 亿美元到 AI / 平台，且长期有回馈现金的记录——仅第四季度就回购 36 亿美元、约 12 年回购了约 40% 的股份——前提是内部没有更好的用途。

关于持久优势：Booking 持有 860 万套非标住宿房源（约为 Airbnb 同品类的 3/4，且连续 5 年增速更快）外加庞大的酒店业务；全球旅行监管日趋严格，是新进入者低估的壁垒。Fogel 真正的担忧在就业：技术一直在替代工作（Booking 的 40 语种人工翻译已悉数让位于机器翻译），但速度——以及那些无法再就业的人，他口中的"50 岁卡车司机"——才是真正的风险。他主张给员工做"AI 素养"再培训，并警告社会不要因恐惧而拒斥技术，指出中国并没有被这种情绪拖住。他的呼吁：一场诚实、不唱衰的对话。
